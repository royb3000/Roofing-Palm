Residential Roofing Questions

Get answers to common questions about residential roofing in South Florida.

Roof lifespan in South Florida depends on the material. Asphalt shingles typically last 15-25 years, metal roofing 40-70 years, tile roofs 50+ years, and cedar shake 20-30 years. The intense sun, salt air, heavy rain, and hurricane winds in our region can accelerate wear, which is why choosing high-quality materials and expert installation is critical. During your free inspection, we will assess your current roof and recommend the best solution for your home and budget.
For maximum hurricane protection, metal roofing is the top choice. Standing seam metal roofs can withstand winds up to 180 mph when properly installed. Concrete and clay tile roofs also offer excellent wind resistance, especially with hurricane clips and adhesive underlayment. We also install impact-resistant asphalt shingles rated for high-wind zones. All of our installations meet or exceed Miami-Dade County wind code requirements, regardless of the material you choose.
Absolutely. A new roof is one of the highest-return home improvements you can make. On average, homeowners recoup 60-70% of the cost at resale, and in South Florida's competitive real estate market, that return can be even higher. A new roof also reduces insurance premiums, improves energy efficiency, and makes your home significantly more attractive to buyers. Many of our customers see their homeowner's insurance drop by 20-40% after installing a new wind-rated roof.
