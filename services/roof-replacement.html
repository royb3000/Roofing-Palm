Roof Replacement

When Does Your Roof Need Replacement?

Even the best-maintained roof has a finite lifespan. South Florida's intense UV radiation, extreme heat, salt air, and annual hurricane season accelerate the aging process significantly. Knowing when to invest in a new roof saves you from escalating repair costs, structural damage, and preventable insurance headaches.

Our licensed inspectors evaluate every factor, from material condition and underlayment integrity to code compliance and energy efficiency, to give you a clear, honest recommendation.

✓ Roof is 20+ years old
✓ Frequent, recurring leaks
✓ Visible sagging or structural issues
✓ Rising energy bills from poor insulation
✓ Widespread shingle or tile deterioration
✓ Major storm or hurricane damage

FAQ

Roof Replacement Questions

Answers to the most common questions homeowners ask about replacing their roof in South Florida.

Most residential roof replacements in Palm Beach County are completed within 1-3 days, depending on the size of your home, roof complexity, and the material being installed. Tile and metal roofs may take slightly longer than shingle roofs. We provide a detailed project timeline during your consultation so you know exactly what to expect each day.
Roof replacement costs depend on several factors including the size of your home, the material you choose, structural requirements, and permit fees. A typical South Florida residential roof replacement ranges from $12,000 to $45,000. Asphalt shingles are the most affordable option, while metal and tile roofs cost more but offer significantly longer lifespans. We provide free, detailed estimates with exact pricing and financing options.
For South Florida's hurricane zone, standing seam metal roofing offers the highest wind resistance, rated up to 180 MPH. Concrete and clay tiles are also excellent choices, providing heavy-duty protection against wind-borne debris. High-quality architectural shingles rated for 130+ MPH winds are a strong, more affordable option. During your consultation, we will recommend the best material for your specific location, budget, and aesthetic preferences.
In most cases, yes. Florida insurance companies commonly offer significant discounts for homes with new, code-compliant roofing systems. A new roof typically qualifies for a wind mitigation report showing improved protection, which can reduce premiums by 15-30% or more. We provide all necessary documentation and work with your insurance company to help you maximize your savings. Many of our customers find that the insurance savings alone offset a substantial portion of their monthly financing payment.
