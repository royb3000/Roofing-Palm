Flat Roof Systems

Types of Flat Roofing We Install

We work with the industry's top flat roofing systems, each engineered for maximum performance in South Florida's demanding climate.

TPO (Thermoplastic)

TPO is one of the most popular flat roofing membranes for commercial properties. Its white reflective surface reduces cooling costs significantly, making it ideal for the Florida sun. Heat-welded seams provide exceptional waterproofing and durability that lasts 20-30 years.

Best for energy efficiency →

EPDM (Rubber)

EPDM rubber roofing is a time-tested single-ply membrane known for exceptional durability and weather resistance. Its flexibility allows it to withstand building movement and temperature changes while providing reliable waterproofing for 25-30+ years.

Best for durability →

Modified Bitumen

Modified bitumen combines the proven reliability of traditional built-up roofing with modern polymer technology. This multi-layer system delivers outstanding waterproofing and puncture resistance, perfect for heavy foot traffic areas and equipment-laden rooftops.

Best for heavy-duty applications →

Built-Up Roofing (BUR)

Built-Up Roofing is the original flat roofing system with over a century of proven performance. Multiple layers of bitumen and reinforcing fabrics create a continuous sealed surface with redundant waterproofing. An excellent choice for large commercial applications.

Best for large commercial roofs →

Maintenance Guide

Flat Roof Maintenance Tips for Florida

South Florida's intense sun, heavy rainfall, and hurricane season demand proactive flat roof maintenance. Following a regular maintenance schedule can significantly extend your roof's lifespan and prevent costly emergency repairs.

Our team recommends these essential maintenance practices for flat roofs in the Florida climate:

✓ Schedule bi-annual professional inspections (before and after hurricane season)
✓ Keep drains, scuppers, and gutters clear of debris to prevent ponding water
✓ Check for and repair membrane punctures, blisters, or seam separation
✓ Apply reflective coatings to extend membrane life and improve energy efficiency
✓ Trim overhanging branches that can damage the roof surface during storms
✓ Inspect flashing around HVAC units, vents, and roof edges after severe weather

FAQ

Flat Roofing Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about flat roofing in South Florida.

Despite the name, flat roofs are not completely level. They are built with a slight slope (typically 1/4 inch per foot) to direct water toward drains, scuppers, or gutters. In South Florida, proper drainage is critical due to heavy rainfall. We install internal drains, edge scuppers, and tapered insulation systems to ensure water never ponds on your roof. Regular drain maintenance is essential, especially during hurricane season, to keep your flat roof performing at its best.
The lifespan of a flat roof in Florida depends on the material and maintenance. TPO and EPDM membranes typically last 20-30 years, while modified bitumen and BUR systems can last 20-25 years. With proper maintenance, including regular inspections and timely repairs, some flat roofs exceed their expected lifespan. Florida's intense UV exposure and storm activity can accelerate wear, which is why we recommend bi-annual professional inspections and high-quality UV-resistant materials.
When properly installed and maintained, flat roofs are no more prone to leaking than pitched roofs. The key is professional installation with proper drainage, high-quality materials, and regular maintenance. Modern flat roofing membranes like TPO and EPDM feature heat-welded or fully adhered seams that create a watertight barrier. The most common causes of flat roof leaks are clogged drains (leading to ponding water), deteriorated flashing, and punctures from foot traffic or falling debris. Our expert installation and maintenance programs address all of these concerns.
Flat roof coatings are liquid-applied membranes (such as silicone, acrylic, or polyurethane) that create a seamless protective layer over your existing roof. They are absolutely worth considering in Florida. Reflective coatings can reduce roof surface temperatures by up to 50 degrees, significantly lowering cooling costs. They also extend your roof's lifespan by protecting the underlying membrane from UV degradation and weathering. Coatings are especially cost-effective when your existing roof is structurally sound but showing signs of surface wear, potentially saving thousands compared to a full replacement.
