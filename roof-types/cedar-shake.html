Cedar Shake Options

Types of Cedar Shakes We Install

We offer a full range of premium cedar shake products, each selected for quality, longevity, and visual impact in the South Florida environment.

Hand-Split Shakes

Hand-split cedar shakes are the most traditional and rustic option, split directly from cedar logs to preserve the natural wood grain. Their rough, textured surface creates dramatic shadow lines and a rugged, artisan appearance that adds extraordinary character and depth to any roofline.

Most rustic and textured →

Taper-Sawn Shakes

Taper-sawn shakes combine the beauty of natural cedar with a more refined, uniform profile. Sawn on both sides for a smoother finish, they offer a tailored look that bridges the gap between rustic shakes and clean-cut shingles. An ideal choice for upscale South Florida residences.

Refined and elegant →

Treated Cedar Shakes

Treated cedar shakes are pressure-treated with fire retardants and preservatives, making them the ideal choice for Florida's climate. These shakes meet stringent fire codes while offering enhanced resistance to mold, mildew, and fungal decay that South Florida's humidity can cause.

Best for Florida climate →

Maintenance Guide

Cedar Shake Maintenance in South Florida

South Florida's high humidity, intense sun, and heavy rainfall create unique challenges for cedar shake roofs. A proactive maintenance program is essential to protect your investment and ensure your cedar shake roof reaches its full 30-40 year lifespan.

Our cedar shake maintenance program is specifically designed for the South Florida climate, addressing humidity-related concerns and preserving the natural beauty of your roof:

✓ Annual professional inspections to catch issues early before they spread
✓ Gentle cleaning to remove mold, mildew, and algae caused by Florida humidity
✓ Preservative oil treatments every 3-5 years to maintain natural oils and repel moisture
✓ Prompt replacement of cracked, split, or curled shakes before damage spreads
✓ Trim nearby vegetation to improve air circulation and reduce moisture retention
✓ Apply fungicide and fire retardant treatments to protect against Florida's climate threats
