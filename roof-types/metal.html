Metal Options

Types of Metal Roofing

We offer a complete range of metal roofing systems, each engineered for maximum performance in South Florida's demanding coastal environment.

Aluminum Roofing

The gold standard for coastal Florida properties. Aluminum is naturally resistant to saltwater corrosion, making it the ideal metal roofing choice for homes near the ocean. Lightweight yet incredibly strong, aluminum roofing panels will never rust, even in the most demanding salt-spray environments. Available in standing seam and interlocking panel profiles with a wide range of factory-applied color finishes.

✓ Best for coastal properties
✓ Naturally corrosion-proof

Steel Roofing

Galvanized and Galvalume steel roofing delivers exceptional strength and impact resistance at a more accessible price point than aluminum or copper. Modern protective coatings and stone-coated steel profiles provide outstanding corrosion resistance while offering the look of traditional tile, slate, or shake. An excellent choice for inland South Florida homes seeking maximum durability and value.

✓ Strongest metal option
✓ Stone-coated styles available

Copper Roofing

The pinnacle of luxury roofing materials. Copper roofs develop a distinctive green patina over time that is prized for its timeless elegance and old-world character. With a potential lifespan exceeding 100 years, copper is the most enduring roofing material available. Frequently used for accent features, turrets, dormers, and bay windows on high-end South Florida estates and historic properties.

✓ 100+ year potential lifespan
✓ Iconic patina develops over time
Why Metal

Advantages of Metal Roofing

Discover why more South Florida homeowners are choosing metal roofing for unbeatable protection, efficiency, and long-term value.

🔥

Fire-Retardant

Metal roofing carries a Class A fire rating, the highest available. Unlike wood or asphalt, metal will not ignite from sparks, lightning, or wildfires, providing an added layer of safety for your family and property.

🔧

Less Maintenance

Metal roofs require virtually no maintenance compared to tile or shingle alternatives. No cracking, curling, or granule loss to worry about. A simple annual inspection and occasional rinse are typically all that is needed.

⏳

Long Lasting

With a 40-70 year expected lifespan, metal roofing outlasts nearly every other roofing material two to three times over. Many metal roofs installed decades ago are still performing flawlessly today.

🌀

Hurricane Resistant

Engineered and tested to withstand wind speeds of 160 mph and beyond, metal roofing is the ultimate choice for hurricane protection. Interlocking panels and concealed fasteners resist uplift forces that would tear apart other materials.

☀

Energy Efficient

Reflective metal panels bounce back solar radiation instead of absorbing it, keeping your attic cooler and reducing air conditioning costs by up to 25%. Many metal roofing products qualify for ENERGY STAR certification.

🌿

Eco-Friendly

Metal roofing is one of the most sustainable building materials available. Most metal roofing contains 25-95% recycled content and is 100% recyclable at end of life, keeping waste out of landfills.

Florida Focused

Why Metal Roofing Is Ideal for Florida

South Florida's climate presents a unique set of challenges that make metal roofing an especially smart investment. From relentless UV exposure and daily afternoon thunderstorms to the ever-present threat of major hurricanes, your roof endures more punishment here than almost anywhere else in the country.

Metal roofing is specifically engineered to handle these extremes. Its reflective surface combats intense solar heat, its sealed panel systems shed torrential rain effortlessly, and its wind ratings far exceed Florida Building Code minimums. For coastal homeowners, corrosion-resistant aluminum and coated steel systems stand up to the salt-laden air that rapidly degrades other materials.

Many Florida insurance providers also offer premium discounts for metal roofs due to their superior wind and impact resistance, meaning your roof upgrade may actually pay for itself over time through lower insurance costs and virtually eliminated maintenance expenses.
